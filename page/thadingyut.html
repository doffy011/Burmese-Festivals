Thadingyut Festival in OCTOBER

Thadingyut is the seventh Myanmar month in (September- October) towards the end of rainy season. Lord Gautama Buddha preached The Abhidamma to His reincarnated mother in Tavatimsa , abode of celestial beings for three Lenten months and returned to the abode of men on the full- moon day of Thadingyut . The King of the celestials created three stairways, gold, silver and ruby for him. Buddha took the middle ruby stairways radiating six hues of aura. The celestials came along by the right gold stairways and the brahmas by the left silver stairways . On account of that, Myanmar Buddhists celebrate Tavatimsa Festival on the full-moon day of Thadingyut by lighting multi-coloured illuminations. For the Sangha it is the time known as Pawayana, which means inviting, entreating, urging.

The Thadingyut Festival, also known as the Festival of Lights, is one of the most important Buddhist celebrations in Myanmar. It takes place on the full moon day of Thadingyut (usually in October) and marks the end of Buddhist Lent (Vassa). The festival commemorates the Buddha’s descent from Tavatimsa Heaven, where he had preached to his mother. It is a time for gratitude, reflection, and merit-making.

People celebrate Thadingyut by lighting candles, oil lamps, and colorful electric lights at homes, streets, and pagodas to welcome the Buddha’s return. It's also a time when young people show respect to parents, teachers, and elders by paying homage and asking for forgiveness. Families gather, offer food and donations to monks, and enjoy festive activities, music, and street fairs. It’s a joyful and meaningful celebration of light, respect, and community.
