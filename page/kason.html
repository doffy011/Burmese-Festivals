Kason Festival in May

The Kason festival, which is held in May, is one of the most significant festivals in Myanmar's religious life. It marks the day when Buddha was born, achieved enlightenment, and died. Furthermore, it's also the day when the Buddha tree rises from the ground as a sign that Gautama Buddha will soon arrive. There are lots of banyan trees in Myanmar, particularly around the pagodas. The sacred Bodhi tree provides a restful environment as well as majestic, elegance, and charm. On Kason's full moon day, thousands of Buddhist devotees anoint the Maha-Bodhi tree with scented water to protect it from dying of drought in May, when temperatures are the highest in Myanmar. Tourists visiting the Kason celebration will have an opportunity to learn about Burmese religious customs and their belief in Buddha.

The Kason Festival is a significant Buddhist celebration in Myanmar, held on the full moon day of the month of Kason (usually in May). It commemorates three important events in the life of Gautama Buddha: his birth, enlightenment, and passing into Nirvanaâ€”all of which are believed to have occurred on this same day. The festival is a time for Buddhists to honor these sacred moments and renew their devotion to Buddhist teachings.

On the day of the Kason Festival, devotees visit pagodas and pour water at the foot of Bodhi trees, which symbolize the tree under which Buddha attained enlightenment. This ritual is meant to show respect and also to prevent the sacred trees from drying out during the hot season. People wear traditional clothes, chant prayers, and participate in merit-making activities like donating food or helping at monasteries.
